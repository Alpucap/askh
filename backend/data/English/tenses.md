# English Tenses

## Present Tenses

### 1. Simple Present Tense
> **Simply:** General present time.

**Formula:** Subject + Verb 1 (s/es)

**Signal Words:** always, every, never, normally, often, sometimes, usually, seldom

**Usage:** Commonly used for facts, habits, and events happening in the present.

**Examples:**
(+) He writes code every day.
(-) He does not write code every day.
(?) Does he write code every day?


### 2. Present Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Happening now.

**Formula:** Subject + am/is/are + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** at the moment, just now, now, right now, listen, look

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions happening right now or temporary situations.

**Examples:**
(+) I am fixing a bug right now.
(-) I am not fixing a bug right now.
(?) Am I fixing a bug right now?


### 3. Present Perfect Tense
> **Simply:** Started in past, relevant now.

**Formula:** Subject + has/have + Verb 3

**Signal Words:** already, ever, just, never, not yet, so far, till now, up to now

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that started in the past and continue to the present, or have just finished with a result in the present.

**Examples:**
(+) She has finished the design.
(-) She has not finished the design.
(?) Has she finished the design?


### 4. Present Perfect Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Started in past, still doing it now.

**Formula:** Subject + has/have + been + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** for, since, the whole week, how long

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that started in the past and are still continuing now, emphasizing the duration.

**Examples:**
(+) We have been developing this app for two months.
(-) We have not been developing this app for two months.
(?) Have we been developing this app for two months?


## Past Tenses

### 5. Simple Past Tense
> **Simply:** Finished in the past.

**Formula:** Subject + Verb 2

**Signal Words:** yesterday, ago, last week, last year, in 2010

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that happened and finished at a specific time in the past.

**Examples:**
(+) They launched the website yesterday.
(-) They did not launch the website yesterday.
(?) Did they launch the website yesterday?


### 6. Past Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Was happening in the past.

**Formula:** Subject + was/were + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** when, while, as long as, at this time yesterday

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that were happening at a specific moment in the past, often interrupted by another action.

**Examples:**
(+) I was drawing pixel art when you called.
(-) I was not drawing pixel art when you called.
(?) Was I drawing pixel art when you called?


### 7. Past Perfect Tense
> **Simply:** The "past" before the "past".

**Formula:** Subject + had + Verb 3

**Signal Words:** before, after, by the time, as soon as

**Usage:** Commonly used for an action that happened before another action in the past.

**Examples:**
(+) He had saved the file before the computer crashed.
(-) He had not saved the file before the computer crashed.
(?) Had he saved the file before the computer crashed?


### 8. Past Perfect Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Duration before a past event.

**Formula:** Subject + had + been + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** for, since, before, when

**Usage:** Commonly used to show that an action started in the past and continued up to another point in the past.

**Examples:**
(+) She had been coding for three hours when the power went out.
(-) She had not been coding for three hours when the power went out.
(?) Had she been coding for three hours when the power went out?


## Future Tenses

### 9. Simple Future Tense
> **Simply:** Will happen later.

**Formula:** Subject + will + Verb 1

**Signal Words:** tomorrow, later, next week, soon, tonight

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that will happen in the future, predictions, or spontaneous decisions.

**Examples:**
(+) I will study English tomorrow.
(-) I will not study English tomorrow.
(?) Will I study English tomorrow?


### 10. Future Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Will be happening at a specific time.

**Formula:** Subject + will + be + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** at this time tomorrow, at 5 PM tomorrow

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

**Examples:**
(+) At 8 PM, I will be working on my project.
(-) At 8 PM, I will not be working on my project.
(?) Will I be working on my project at 8 PM?


### 11. Future Perfect Tense
> **Simply:** Will be done by then.

**Formula:** Subject + will + have + Verb 3

**Signal Words:** by next week, by tomorrow, by the time

**Usage:** Commonly used for actions that will be completed before a specific time in the future.

**Examples:**
(+) I will have finished the course by next month.
(-) I will not have finished the course by next month.
(?) Will I have finished the course by next month?


### 12. Future Perfect Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Duration up to a future point.

**Formula:** Subject + will + have + been + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** for, by the end of this year

**Usage:** Commonly used to emphasize the duration of an action that will continue up to a specific point in the future.

**Examples:**
(+) By December, I will have been working here for two years.
(-) By December, I will not have been working here for two years.
(?) Will I have been working here for two years by December?


## Past Future (Conditional) Tenses

### 13. Simple Past Future Tense
> **Simply:** Future from the past perspective.

**Formula:** Subject + would + Verb 1

**Signal Words:** the next day (in past context), if

**Usage:** Commonly used to express an idea in the past that something would happen in the future, or for conditional sentences (Type 2).

**Examples:**
(+) He said he would join the team.
(-) He said he would not join the team.
(?) Did he say he would join the team?


### 14. Past Future Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Would be happening (imaginary).

**Formula:** Subject + would + be + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** if, when

**Usage:** Commonly used to show an action that would be in progress at a past time, often in conditional sentences.

**Examples:**
(+) I would be sleeping if you didn't call.
(-) I would not be sleeping if you didn't call.
(?) Would I be sleeping if you didn't call?


### 15. Past Future Perfect Tense
> **Simply:** Would have been done (but wasn't).

**Formula:** Subject + would + have + Verb 3

**Signal Words:** if, by last month

**Usage:** Commonly used to talk about actions that would have been completed in the past if a condition had been met (Conditional Type 3).

**Examples:**
(+) I would have passed the test if I had studied.
(-) I would not have passed the test if I hadn't studied.
(?) Would I have passed the test if I had studied?


### 16. Past Future Perfect Continuous Tense
> **Simply:** Duration in an imaginary past.

**Formula:** Subject + would + have + been + Verb-ing

**Signal Words:** for, if

**Usage:** Commonly used to talk about the duration of an action that would have been happening in the past if a condition had been met.

**Examples:**
(+) She would have been waiting for an hour if I hadn't arrived.
(-) She would not have been waiting for an hour if I hadn't arrived.
(?) Would she have been waiting for an hour if I hadn't arrived?
